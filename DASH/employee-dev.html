Training & Seminar

Skills development and progressive learning is vital in DASH in order to attain its goal to excellence. Indulging in trainings, workshops and seminars are beneficial to the growth of the company and a great help to individual development as well.

First Aid Training

2024

Empowering Employees Life-Saving Skills April 23-24, 2024

This year, 30 selected employees were chosen for a hands-on first aid training workshop aimed at providing participants with essential life-saving skills. Hosted in-house, the activity gathered employees from various departments to learn how to respond effectively in emergencies.

The workshop, led by certified first aid instructors from ERUF, covered a range of critical topics, such as CPR, wound care, and how to deal with common injuries. This session was a reminder of how crucial first aid skills are in building safer communities—where prompt and informed action can help save lives.

2019

Project Management Training-Workshop (PMTW) July 2-4, 2019 and July 16 – 18, 2019

DASH recently concluded two batches of Project Management Training-Workshop (PMTW) last July 2-4, 2019 and July 16 – 18, 2019, respectively. It aimed to enhance, if not develop, the participants' understanding of project management knowledge area.

Fifty-five employees from different department’s participated in the training-workshop. It was conducted by Ms. Ma. Luisa Ramirez, a Project Management Professional accredited by Project Management Institute (PMI) and a certified Six Sigma Master Black Belter.

With the emerging expansion in contracting projects with new clients, coupled with the drive for continuous improvement, the PMTW was timely to equip the leaders with the knowledge, tools and basic skills in managing projects successfully. It covered the importance of managing core constraints of a project – scope, schedule, cost and quality. It also tackled contract management and provided essential tips for effective communication – a crucial skill in leadership and project management.

All things considered, all it takes discipline for projects to be successful.

Below are some of the feedback of the participants:
“As a DASH employee who has been working in the company mostly on the technical side, the project management training was very helpful… This made me realize the importance of good project management.” – Tryzha, Civil Department

“…The most important lesson that I learned during the training is that the planning should be taken carefully for the execution to go smooth as possible…” – Wayne, IT Department

“…enabled the participants to organize/ arrange the learning they acquired from the years of working on multiple projects in the company in line with the fundamentals of project management…” – Jobert, Process Department

Dash Chess Tournament

The University Challenge 2019
August 31, 2019

On August 31, 2019, the Dash Engineering Chess Circle held the first ever Dash Chess Tournament – the University Challenge 2019 at the University of the Visayas—Main Campus.
It was a one-day tourney open to all bona fide engineering students from various universities in Cebu.
It aimed to gather chess enthusiasts wherein they can showcase their skills and hone their talents while fostering camaraderie with other players from other schools.

The University of the Visayas team won as the Champion while the contenders from University of Cebu—Main and University of Cebu – LM campuses grabbed the 2nd and 4th places, respectively.
The Dash Chess Circle players came in 3rd.
The winners were awarded with money prizes and certificates from Dash Engineering Philippines, Inc.
Mr. Shiga, the Company President, hopes the Company will hold such kind of tournament yearly.

Company-wide Fun Run for Ocean Conservation

July 13, 2019
An estimated 250 Dash employees took part in the company-wide Fun Run to promote ocean conservation while also fostering health awareness and a stress-free lifestyle among employees at Family Park, Talamban, Cebu City, last July 13, 2019 (Saturday).

OSHC initiated this event due to the increasing plastic waste levels in the world’s ocean. Philippines is one of the top three contributors of mismanaged plastic waste, according to a study published by The Wall Street Journal.

Employees took part in the 5K and 10K categories, respectively. In order to finish the runs, employee-participants must pass at least three out of the six obstacles. They could also choose to pass all of the hurdles.

The proceeds of the activity will be donated to the People and the Sea, a non-profit, non-government organization based in Malapascua Island, Cebu. This organization is promoting capacity building and community-based marine resource management that fosters the sustainable use and conservation of coastal resources as a way to alleviate poverty and increase the resilience of the coastal communities.
